The Warsaw Ghetto Uprising

April 19, 1943 - May 16, 1943

The Warsaw Ghetto Uprising is a story of hope and resilience in the dark and horrific narrative of the Holocaust. During this revolt, hundreds of residents of the Warsaw ghetto in Poland with little to no chance of surviving successfully rebelled and fended off German troops for almost a month. While the rebels were eventually beaten and the ghetto destroyed by May 16, 1943, this seemingly impossible feat at the Warsaw ghetto exceeded the fighter's greatest expectations and continues to inspire people today.
